The Crisis

The Fragmented Reality

Modern healthcare suffers from critical data silos that cost lives and delay treatment. Current systems are "Digital Islands" that don't talk to each other.

Medical History Gap

Doctors spend 40% of consultation time just gathering files. 30% of critical history is lost during referrals, leading to redundant tests and clinical errors.

Inventory Paralysis

Clinic stock is managed on paper. Pharmacies have no visibility into local demand. This causes life-saving drugs to expire while patients go without.

The Diagnostic Lag

Patients wait days for physical reports to be hand-carried. This "Time-to-Result" gap is the primary cause of delayed critical care intervention.

Logistical Chaos

Needy patients struggle to find wheelchairs or oxygen in emergencies. Local rentals are undocumented and difficult to locate under pressure.

THE BOTTOM LINE

Fragmented data means slower care, higher costs, and increased risk of preventable mortality.
